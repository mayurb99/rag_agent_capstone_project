GENERAL MEDICAL FAQ - CITY CLINIC

Q: What are the symptoms of diabetes?
A: Common symptoms of diabetes include frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, extreme hunger, blurry vision, slow-healing sores or bruises, fatigue, and frequent infections. Type 1 diabetes symptoms often develop quickly while Type 2 symptoms develop more slowly.

Q: What is hypertension?
A: Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high (typically 130/80 mmHg or higher). It is known as the silent killer because it often has no symptoms. Untreated hypertension can lead to heart attack, stroke, and kidney damage.

Q: What are common side effects of metformin?
A: Metformin is commonly used for Type 2 diabetes. Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach pain, and a metallic taste in the mouth. These side effects are usually mild and often improve over time. Serious but rare side effects include lactic acidosis.

Q: What should I do if I have chest pain?
A: Chest pain can be a sign of a heart attack. If you experience sudden, severe chest pain, especially with shortness of breath, sweating, or pain radiating to your arm or jaw, call emergency services (108 in India) immediately. Not all chest pain is cardiac — it can also be caused by acid reflux, muscle strain, or anxiety.

Q: What is a routine health checkup?
A: A routine health checkup (also called annual physical) includes measurement of blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol levels, body mass index (BMI), and review of vaccinations. It also includes a physical examination, blood tests, and urine analysis. Regular checkups help detect problems early.

Q: What are the symptoms of a heart attack?
A: Heart attack symptoms include chest pain or pressure, pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or back, shortness of breath, cold sweat, nausea or vomiting, lightheadedness, and sudden fatigue. Women may experience atypical symptoms like unusual fatigue, nausea, or back pain without chest pain.

Q: What is cholesterol and what levels are normal?
A: Cholesterol is a waxy substance found in your blood. Normal levels: Total cholesterol below 200 mg/dL, LDL (bad) cholesterol below 100 mg/dL, HDL (good) cholesterol above 40 mg/dL for men and above 50 for women, Triglycerides below 150 mg/dL.

Q: What is the difference between Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes?
A: Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin. It typically begins in childhood and requires insulin therapy. Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body doesn't use insulin properly (insulin resistance). It is more common in adults and can often be managed with lifestyle changes and oral medications.

Q: What should I know about paracetamol (acetaminophen)?
A: Paracetamol is commonly used to relieve pain and reduce fever. The standard adult dose is 500 mg to 1 gram every 4-6 hours, not exceeding 4 grams per day. Overdose can cause serious liver damage. Do not combine with alcohol. It is generally safe when taken as directed.

Q: What is asthma?
A: Asthma is a chronic condition where airways narrow and swell, producing extra mucus, making breathing difficult. Symptoms include shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing, and chest tightness. Triggers include allergens, smoke, exercise, cold air, and respiratory infections. Treatment includes inhalers (bronchodilators and corticosteroids).

CLINIC POLICIES

Appointment Policy: Patients must arrive 10 minutes before their scheduled appointment. Late arrivals beyond 15 minutes may need to be rescheduled. Cancellations must be made at least 2 hours in advance.

Emergency Policy: For life-threatening emergencies, call 108 immediately or proceed to the nearest emergency room. Our clinic handles non-emergency outpatient consultations only.

Prescription Policy: Prescriptions are valid for 30 days from the date of issue. Repeat prescriptions require a follow-up consultation for chronic medications.

Insurance: We accept major health insurance plans. Please carry your insurance card and photo ID to every appointment.
